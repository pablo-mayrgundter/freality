Hexagonal force automata

Wolfram models a fluid as particle interactions on a hexagonal field (p. 378).

Each cell has incident forces to it at the beginning of each step. The automata evolution rule specifies how the cell transfers these forces outwards by the end of the step. Wolfram seems to pick a particular rule which transfers the forces most symmetrically for this configuration, and assumes that the count of incident fields is half, or 3/6.

Solids within the flow are represented as an additional rule reflecting incident vectors.
